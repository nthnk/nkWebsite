Experience. Variety.

Eastminster United Church

July 2017 - August 2017

During the second half of my recent summer, I began working as a planning assistant, general
maintenance worker, and sunday school teacher at Toronto's largest United Church.

I worked as a general maintenance worker for the first half of my work contract and as a planning
assistant the second half of the work contract. Maintenance consisted of large projects such as
independently building an outdoor drainage system. Planning was done electronically using a
planning software that the church had installed on my laptop. I helped with room booking and
planning schedules.

On Sundays I taught Sunday school to young children from the ages of 4-13.

Alarmguard Security Sales Team

June 2017

I spent a month learning sales techniques and improving my communication skills by going door to
door in Ajax hoping to sell quality alarm systems.

At the end of June I quit my job with Alarmguard Sales because travelling to Ajax each day was
consuming much of my time. However, I truly valued how much I was able to improve my
interpersonal and customer service skills through hands-on experience and/or by observing more
experienced sales workers.

Hatch Canada

April 2017 - June 2017

After returning from Waterloo, I was able to get a position with Hatch Canada as a child Javascript
Coach. Once/twice a week I would visit a school or the Hatch centre location downtown to guide
children in using the Hatch online platform to complete activities and learn how to use P5JS, a
library of Javascript.

My contract with Hatch ended when the elementary school term ended, and I decided to apply to
new jobs to gain a wider variety of job experience. Hatch started my interest in coding and taught
me the basics. I really enjoyed working with the children while also learning about something I was
interested in.

CNE BLC Foodtruck Team Member

August 2016

Near the end of my grade 12 summer, I worked with the vendor Escar Entertainment at the BLC
foodtruck serving CNE visitors with roasted corn, fast food, freshly squeezed lemonade, and deep
fried chocolate treats.

Although I worked tiring 9 hour shifts, six days a week, working at the CNE was wonderful because it
helped me improve my customer service skills while allowing me to experience a fast-paced
working environment. I met many great new friends and really enjoyed working with all my
managers and coworkers at the CNE!

Nick's No Frills

November 2015 - March 2016

My very first job was with Nick's No Frills as a cashier. I learned all the produce codes and helped
customers with checking out their groceries. It introduced me to customer service as well as
allowing me to experience handling money and more responsibilities.

I resigned from No Frills in order to focus on my schoolwork in grade 12 and getting into University.
I truly treasure the opportunity I was given by Nick to experience my very first part-time job!
